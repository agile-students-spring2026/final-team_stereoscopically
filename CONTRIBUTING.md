# Contributing

This document outlines the team values, development workflow, contribution rules, and setup instructions that all contributors are expected to follow to maintain a productive and organized development process.

## Team Norms

Team norms define the commitments that members make to ensure effective collaboration and accountability.

### Team Values

Important agreements so that everyone knows what is expected of them in terms of responsibility.

- Members will first try to find help from online and one another before reaching out to people in the class and eventually the professor.
- When there is a disagreement, members will put it to a vote or push the discussion to a further date.
- If a member is failing to deliver on their obligations, it will first be resolved internally (ideally during stand-up), and eventually will be brought to the professor if it remains consistent.
- Members must try to respond to messages directed at them ASAP, whether that be through a reaction or an actual message.

#### Collaboration

- Team members will make their best attempts to maintain strong communication through messaging chats and calls.
- Members will provide their weekly availability to allow for easier organization.

#### Expectations and Accountability

Team members agree to the following expectations:

- Communicate clearly and respectfully.
- Ask for help when blocked.
- Assist teammates when possible.
- Deliver assigned work within sprint timelines.
- Attempt to respond to team messages within 24 hours on weekdays (or react if you can’t respond fully yet).

#### Conflict Resolution

If conflicts occur:

- Team members should first discuss the issue directly amongst each other.
- If a solution cannot be reached, the team will bring it up in stand-up.
- If the issue still cannot be resolved, the team will report the issue to the course staff (TA/professor).

## Sprint Cadence

A sprint should take around 2–3 weeks to complete.

## Weekly Standups

- Standups occur once a week on **Tuesdays at 3:30pm EST** and should be limited to **30 minutes** (may end early if all items are covered).
- Members are expected to be present synchronously.
- Every member is required to send their own stand-up notes, which will reflect attendance.
- Standup notes should be posted in the team chat thread before the meeting (or immediately after if needed).
- Members will not cover for other members who do not participate.
- Members who miss more than **2 standups in a row** or do not make progress on their tasks will be documented, spoken to first, and then reported to the professor.
